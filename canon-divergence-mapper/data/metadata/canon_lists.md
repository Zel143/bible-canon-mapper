# Canon Lists by Tradition

Reference list to seed `data/metadata/canon_lists.csv` once formalized into structured data.

## Protestant Canon (66 books)
- Old Testament (39): follows the Masoretic Text ordering/content
- New Testament (27): shared across all three traditions

## Catholic Canon (73 books)
- Old Testament (46): includes the Protestant 39 plus 7 deuterocanonical books:
  - Tobit, Judith, 1 Maccabees, 2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach (Ecclesiasticus), Baruch
  - Plus additions to Esther and Daniel (e.g., Prayer of Azariah, Susanna, Bel and the Dragon)
- New Testament (27): same as Protestant

## Orthodox Canon (varies by jurisdiction, typically 76–81 books)
- Includes all Catholic deuterocanonical books
- Plus additional texts depending on jurisdiction: 1 Esdras, Prayer of Manasseh, Psalm 151,
  3 Maccabees, and (in some Slavonic/Ethiopian traditions) 4 Maccabees, Jubilees, Enoch

## Key Historical Decision Points (to research further in historical_notes.md)
- Council of Rome (382 AD) / Council of Hippo (393) / Council of Carthage (397) — early canon affirmations
- Jerome's Vulgate and his *veritas hebraica* position on the Hebrew-only OT canon
- Council of Trent (1546) — Catholic Church formally defines the 73-book canon in response to the Reformation
- Luther's Bible (1534) — relocates deuterocanonical books to an "Apocrypha" section, questions their canonicity
- Westminster Confession (1647) — Protestant confessional rejection of Apocrypha as Scripture
